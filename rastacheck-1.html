How the signal works

Three colors. One clear decision.

Every stretch of road on RastaCheck carries a status, set from resident reports and confirmed by the field verification team.

Green spot

Surface is in good condition. No meaningful potholes, cracking, or drainage issues reported or found on inspection.

Go ahead — no detour needed.

Yellow spot

Manageable but worth caution — patchy surface, minor potholes, or narrowing. Passable, especially in daylight and at moderate speed.

Proceed with caution — reduce speed.

Red / high alert spot

Confirmed unsafe by at least two field verifiers — deep potholes, missing surface, or standing water. Auto-escalated to the government.

Avoid — take the suggested alternate route.

Makes a report easier to verify

  • Name a landmark or junction, not just a road — verifiers need to find it fast.
  • Say whether it's the whole stretch or one specific spot.
  • Mention if it's worse at night, in rain, or during a particular time of day.
  • Reports are shown with your name — false or exaggerated reports affect your credibility score on future reports.

Government escalation

Public accountability tracker

Confirmed red spots are filed as tickets to the relevant road authority. Status is public — anyone can see what's been acknowledged, what's in progress, and what's been fixed.

Suggestions to strengthen this

Ideas worth building next

Photo + GPS on every report

Require a photo and auto-captured GPS pin instead of typed location — makes verification faster and much harder to fake.

Reporter credibility score

Track each reporter's accuracy over time (confirmed vs. rejected reports) and weight new reports from high-credibility users higher in the queue.

SLA clock on government tickets

Give each severity level a public response-time target (e.g. red spots: acknowledge in 3 days) so delays are visible, not just statuses.

Route-aware navigation warning

Integrate with maps so a red spot automatically triggers a reroute suggestion for anyone navigating through it, not just a listing on the site.

Duplicate detection

Cluster reports within ~100m of each other into one spot so the same pothole isn't logged (and escalated) as five separate tickets.

Verifier rotation & conflict-of-interest checks

Rotate which of the three verifiers reviews which area, and flag if a verifier has a personal connection to a report.
